UAE Corporate Tax: Year-End Compliance Checklist for DIFC Companies

22 January 20265 min read

With the UAE corporate tax regime now firmly established, DIFC-registered companies must ensure their year-end compliance obligations are met. We set out the key steps to take before your financial year closes.

The UAE Corporate Tax regime, which came into effect for financial years beginning on or after 1 June 2023, applies to businesses operating across the UAE including those in the DIFC. As financial year-end approaches, DIFC-registered companies should ensure they have addressed the following key compliance requirements.

Understanding Your Tax Status

Before addressing year-end obligations, it is important to confirm your company's tax status under the UAE Corporate Tax Law. DIFC entities may qualify as a Qualifying Free Zone Person (QFZP), which provides a zero per cent corporate tax rate on qualifying income, subject to meeting specific conditions.

To qualify as a QFZP, a DIFC company must:

  • Maintain adequate substance in the DIFC
  • Derive income from qualifying activities
  • Not have elected to be subject to the standard corporate tax rate
  • Comply with transfer pricing rules where applicable
  • Meet de minimis thresholds for non-qualifying income

Year-End Compliance Checklist

Financial Statements

Ensure your financial statements for the relevant tax period are prepared in accordance with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) or another acceptable accounting standard. Financial statements must accurately reflect the company's income, expenses and balance sheet position.

Tax Return Preparation

UAE Corporate Tax returns must be filed within nine months of the end of the relevant tax period. Confirm your filing deadline and ensure sufficient time is allocated for return preparation, review and submission through the Federal Tax Authority (FTA) EmaraTax portal.

Transfer Pricing Documentation

If your DIFC company transacts with related parties, ensure that transfer pricing documentation is in place and that transactions are conducted on an arm's length basis. Master file and local file requirements may apply depending on the size and structure of your group.

Economic Substance

While the UAE's Economic Substance Regulations (ESR) have been largely superseded by the Corporate Tax regime, certain filing obligations under ESR may still apply for earlier financial periods. Confirm whether any outstanding ESR notifications or reports are required.

VAT Compliance

Ensure all VAT returns for the financial year have been filed and any outstanding VAT liabilities have been settled. Review input tax recovery positions and confirm that VAT grouping arrangements, if applicable, remain appropriate.

Dividend and Distribution Planning

Review any planned dividend distributions or profit repatriation in light of UAE Corporate Tax implications. Dividends paid by UAE resident companies to shareholders are generally exempt from corporate tax, but specific conditions apply.
