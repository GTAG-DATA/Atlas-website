DIFC 2026: New Fund Regulations and What They Mean for Asset Managers

10 March 20266 min read

The DIFC Authority has introduced significant updates to its fund regulations framework in 2026. We examine the key changes and what they mean for asset managers operating in the centre.

The Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) has updated its collective investment funds framework, introducing changes that affect how asset managers structure, register and operate investment funds within the centre.

Key Changes to the DIFC Funds Framework

The 2026 amendments build on the DIFC's ongoing efforts to align its regulatory environment with international best practice. The Dubai Financial Services Authority (DFSA) has revised its Collective Investment Rules (CIR) to introduce greater clarity on fund categories, investor eligibility and ongoing disclosure obligations.

Exempt Funds

Exempt Funds remain available for sophisticated and institutional investors, but the 2026 amendments introduce clearer guidance on the maximum number of unitholders and the types of assets that may be held within an exempt structure. Fund managers should review their existing exempt fund documentation against the updated CIR requirements.

Qualified Investor Funds

Qualified Investor Funds (QIFs) continue to offer a lighter regulatory touch for funds targeting professional investors. The 2026 changes clarify the definition of a Qualified Investor and introduce updated subscription thresholds, which fund managers and administrators should review carefully.

Public Funds

For those operating or considering public funds, the DFSA has updated its prospectus requirements and ongoing reporting obligations. The amendments aim to bring DIFC public fund disclosure standards in line with those applied in leading international financial centres.

What Asset Managers Should Do Now

Asset managers operating in the DIFC should carry out a full review of their existing fund documentation, including constitutional documents, offering materials and investor agreements, to ensure alignment with the 2026 amendments.

Key action points include:

  • Review fund categorisation against the updated DFSA definitions
  • Confirm investor eligibility criteria and subscription documentation remain current
  • Update offering memoranda and marketing materials where required
  • Ensure ongoing reporting and disclosure obligations are met under the revised framework
  • Consult with your DFSA-authorised representative or compliance adviser on any structural changes required
